Detailed Description of Drew

Drew's primary mode of living is focused externally, where he takes things in primarily via his intuition. His secondary mode is internal, where he deals with things rationally and logically.

With Extraverted Intuition dominating his personality, Drew's primary interest in life is understanding the world and sussing out possibilities in it. He is constantly absorbing ideas and images about the situations presented in his life. Using his intuition to process this information, Drew is usually extremely quick and accurate in his ability to size up a situation. With the exception of his ENFP cousin, Drew has a deeper understanding of his environment than any of the other types.

This ability to intuitively understand people and situations puts Drew at a distinct advantage in life. He generally understands things quickly and with great depth. Accordingly, Drew is quite flexible and adapts well to a wide range of tasks. He is good at most anything that interests him. As he grows and further develops his intuitive abilities and insights, Drew becomes very aware that exciting possibilities exist all around him.

Drew is an idea person. His perceptive abilities cause him to see possibilities everywhere. He gets excited and enthusiastic about his ideas and is able to spread that enthusiasm to others. In this way, he gets the support needed to fulfill his visions.

Drew is less interested in developing plans of action or making decisions than he is in generating possibilities and ideas. Following through on the implementation of an idea is usually a chore. For Drew, this sometimes results in a habit of never finishing what he starts. When Drew has not developed his Thinking process, he may enthusiastically jump from idea to idea without following through. He needs to take care to think through his ideas fully in order to take advantage of them.

Drew's auxiliary process of Introverted Thinking drives his decision-making process. Although Drew is more interested in absorbing information than in making decisions, he is quite rational and logical when reaching conclusions. When he applies Thinking to his Intuitive perceptions, the outcome can be very powerful. A well-developed Drew is extremely visionary, inventive, and enterprising in his quest for uncovering truth in the world around him.

Drew is a fluent conversationalist, mentally quick, and enjoys verbal sparring with others. He loves to debate issues and may even switch sides sometimes just for the love of debate. When expressing his underlying principles, however, Drew may feel awkward and speak abruptly and intensely.

This personality style is sometimes referred to as the "lawyer type," someone who quickly and accurately understands a situation and objectively and logically acts upon it using their auxiliary Introverted Thinking function. If Drew were tasked with defending someone who committed a crime, he might take advantage of quirks in the law to get his client off the hook. If he actually won the case, he would see his actions as completely fair and proper to the situation because they were lawful—the guilt or innocence of the client would be less relevant. If this type of reasoning goes completely unchecked, Drew may be perceived by others as unethical or dishonest. Because he does not naturally consider the personal, human element in decision-making, Drew should take care to notice the subjective side of situations. This is a potential problem area for someone like Drew. Although his logical abilities lend strength and purpose, they may also isolate him from his feelings and from the social empathy of the culture in which he lives.

The least developed area for Drew is Sensing. He may struggle with taking care of the details of life. Conversely, under stress, Drew may lose his ability to generate possibilities and become obsessed with minor details. These details may seem extremely important at the time, but usually are not. When the stress fades, the obsession with details will fade as well.

In general, Drew is an upbeat visionary. He highly values knowledge and spends much of his life seeking a higher understanding. Drew lives in a world of possibilities and becomes excited about concepts, challenges, and difficulties. When presented with a problem, he is good at improvising and quickly comes up with a creative solution. Creative, clever, fun, curious, and theoretical, Drew has a broad range of possibilities in life.

What Success Means to Drew

Drew is motivated by possibility. He is drawn to the unlikely intersections and novel combinations that others often overlook. In his world, something is always blooming, shifting, or waiting to be reinvented. His energy comes from a never-ending drive to disrupt the status quo—whether by flipping conventional models, remixing patterns, or trailblazing paths no one else sees.

For Drew, success isn't about tradition or stability. It's found in the “aha” moment, in the spark of a new vision, in boldly chasing the next frontier—be it an idea, invention, or adventure. His past is merely a springboard; he rarely lingers in yesterday when tomorrow holds something unexplored. He thrives on impact, innovation, and the thrill of what could be.

Encouraging Drew's Growth

To thrive, Drew needs people who see and support his potential. Those close to him—friends, collaborators, and especially romantic partners—should recognize the unique abilities that make Drew both remarkable and restless. The most fulfilling relationships for Drew are those that invite growth and offer reflection.

  • He sees multiple angles in any situation and appreciates differing points of view.
  • He easily imagines novel solutions that others haven’t considered.
  • He responds quickly and insightfully when something captures his interest.
  • He notices connections and patterns others overlook.
  • He is an energetic conversationalist who holds his own in any debate.
  • He brings out the best in others and spots their untapped potential.

Growth Through Challenges

Every strength, when unchecked, can become a weakness. Drew's greatest asset—his hunger for what’s next—can also leave him scattered or impulsive. Those who partner with him romantically or professionally can gently help him slow down, reflect, and evaluate the consequences of bold ideas before leaping.

  • He may lose interest in relationships or projects once their potential has been realized.
  • He can underestimate people who move slowly or prioritize stability.
  • He sometimes misses emotional cues or underestimates the need for reassurance.
  • He may leap into risk without attending to important details.
  • He is sometimes so confident in his brilliance that he overlooks practical or ethical blind spots.

How to Support Drew

If you love or work closely with Drew, your presence matters. Encourage him to pause before action, reflect before moving forward, and think through how his decisions affect others. The most rewarding connections for Drew are with those who ground his imagination without stifling it, who remind him that change can also happen through stability, intimacy, and thoughtful commitment.

  • Invite him to process ideas out loud—but help him follow through.
  • Celebrate his innovation while offering realism and emotional insight.
  • Gently help him prioritize people and plans over possibilities alone.
  • Remind him that not every transformation needs to be revolutionary.

Ten Rules to Grow With Drew

  • Encourage his creativity: Let him explore, invent, and reimagine.
  • Help him finish: Be patient but firm when things need completion.
  • Talk it out: Let him share what he sees—he learns best aloud.
  • Ground him: Celebrate the simple joys that support his boldness.
  • Expand his empathy: Show how others think and feel differently.
  • Offer structure: Gently show the power of systems and consistency.
  • Challenge his limits: Growth often lies just outside his comfort zone.
  • Mirror feelings: Help him recognize and express his emotions.
  • Let him lead—but with love: Invite his brilliance without enabling detachment.
  • Keep it real: Celebrate his optimism while staying grounded in what matters.
